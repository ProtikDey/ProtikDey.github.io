I am second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Texas at San Antonio, with three years of full-stack engineering experience in Fintech before that. My research asks a simple question with a hard answer: how does trust actually form — and break — when blind and low vision users rely on generative AI for everyday tasks?

02 Research Focus

My research centers on making generative AI genuinely usable for blind and low-vision (BLV) people — not just technically available, but accessible, trustworthy, and privacy-aware in everyday interaction. Generative AI is increasingly woven into how people write, search, navigate, and get things done, yet these tools are rarely designed with screen-reader users in mind. I study how BLV people actually experience these systems: where interactions break down with assistive technology, what makes a response feel dependable or doubtful, and how people weigh the convenience of AI assistance against control over their personal information.

Building on that understanding, I work on translating BLV users' experiences and needs into concrete interaction design guidance — so that the next generation of generative and agentic AI tools is built with accessibility, trust, and privacy as foundations rather than afterthoughts. This work sits at the intersection of human-computer interaction, accessibility, and applied machine learning, combining qualitative methods with human-centered evaluation of AI systems.
